## Get oriented on the science

You don't have to know it all on day one, but these are great places to build intuition:

- [Why a muon collider?](/posts/why/) — the big picture and why now.
- [Physics & Theory](/posts/physics/) · [Accelerator R&D](/posts/accelerator/) · [Detector R&D](/posts/detector/) · [Software & Computing](/posts/reconstruction/)
- [Big Questions](/misconceptions/) — the honest answers to the questions everyone asks.
- [Resources](/resources/) — talks, papers, and tutorials to go deeper.

## Tips for newcomers

- **Ask questions.** Seminars and meetings are friendly, and "basic" questions are often the best ones.
- **You can contribute early.** Students and early-career researchers do real, visible work here.
- **Not sure who to talk to?** Email [usmcc-coord@fnal.gov](mailto:usmcc-coord@fnal.gov) and we'll connect you with someone in your area.
