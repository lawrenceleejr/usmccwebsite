The USMCC is run by and for its members. Day-to-day direction comes from an **elected
leadership team**, guided by an appointed **Leadership Strategy Group**, and area representatives
coordinate the technical work. Our scope and processes are set out in the
collaboration [charter](https://drive.google.com/file/d/1y5Z5OHoUTwrPT8yMk7YV4Ch98EswjqoJ/view?usp=share_link).

## Leadership

Our leadership was elected by the membership in August 2025 — the collaboration's first
elected team, succeeding the interim Coordinating Committee that guided the effort from the
2021 Snowmass process onward.

## Areas of work

The collaboration's technical effort spans four areas — accelerator, detector, physics, and
software — each represented in the leadership. To get involved in an area, reach out to the
[coordination team](mailto:usmcc-coord@fnal.gov) or the relevant representative.

## Charter

The [collaboration charter](https://drive.google.com/file/d/1y5Z5OHoUTwrPT8yMk7YV4Ch98EswjqoJ/view?usp=share_link)
sets out our mission, membership, and how leadership is elected and operates. In short: the
USMCC is an open collaboration; membership is voluntary and free; leadership is elected by
the members; and the Leadership Strategy Group provides continuity and strategic guidance.

## Past leadership

The interim Coordinating Committee and its focus-area conveners steered the effort from
2021 until the 2025 election. You can find the full roster in our
[leadership archive](/contact/leadership_archive/).
