## Our mission

The US Muon Collider Collaboration exists to make a **10 TeV-scale muon collider** a
reality — coordinating the accelerator, detector, physics, and software R&D across US
institutions, and working hand in hand with international partners to bring the energy
frontier back to US soil.

A muon collider is the only near-future technology that can reach these energies
affordably and compactly. Getting there is a two-decade, community-wide effort. Our job is
to organize that effort in the US: to align the work, grow the people, and make the case.

## Our goals as a collaboration

- **Coordinate US R&D** across accelerator, detector, physics, and software so the effort adds up to more than its parts.
- **Advance the fastest path to the energy frontier**, in partnership with the [International Muon Collider Collaboration (IMCC)](/otherorgs/imcc/) and the [national-lab study group](/otherorgs/nationallabstudygroup/).
- **Advocate** for sustained muon-collider R&D in the US strategy and planning process, building on the P5 recommendation.
- **Grow and train the community** — welcoming newcomers, supporting students and early-career researchers, and building the expertise a 20-year program needs.
- **Pursue the opportunity of a Fermilab-sited collider**, leveraging planned upgrades to the proton complex.
